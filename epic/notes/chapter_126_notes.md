# Chapter 126 Notes: The Old World Monkeys
*Aries 14 - Pre-Human Animal Chapters - Stage V (Primates)*

## Arc Overview
- **Chapter 14 of 17**: Pre-Human Animal Chapters (Cenozoic arc)
- **Stage V**: Primates (Chapters 124-128)
- **Timeline**: Miocene-Pliocene (~23-2.6 million years ago)
- **Location**: Africa and Asia

## Major Events
### Cercopithecoid Success
- **Narrow-nosed monkeys**: Old World primate radiation
- **Terrestrial adaptation**: Many species moving to ground
- **Baboon evolution**: Large, powerful ground-dwellers
- **Macaque expansion**: Spreading across Asia

### Dietary Specialization
- **Leaf-eating colobus**: Specialized stomach for tough vegetation
- **Omnivorous baboons**: Flexible diets including meat
- **Cheek pouches**: Food storage during foraging
- **Dental adaptation**: Teeth specialized for different foods

### Social Complexity
- **Multi-male groups**: Complex dominance hierarchies
- **Female coalitions**: Cooperative alliances
- **Grooming networks**: Social bonding through mutual care
- **Territorial behavior**: Group defense of resources

### Ground Transition
- **Savanna adaptation**: Open woodland and grassland living
- **Predator pressure**: Dealing with ground-based threats
- **Locomotion changes**: Walking and running on ground
- **Group coordination**: Enhanced cooperation for safety

## Key Characters
- **Papio**: The Baboon, ground lord
- **Macaca**: The Macaque, Asian wanderer
- **Colobus**: The Leaf-Eater, forest specialist
- **Cercopithecus**: The Guenon, colorful forest dweller

## Themes
- Transition from trees to ground
- Social complexity as survival strategy
- Dietary flexibility enabling success
- Foundation for human ancestors
