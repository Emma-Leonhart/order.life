- **Position in 29-chapter cycle**: Chapter 15 (Central Extinction Anchor)

- **Calendar Alignment**: Most frequent Easter date in Gaian calendar system
- **Mythic Role**: The archetypal Easter event - central "passion" of the 29-chapter cycle

## Successive Civilizations Theme
- **Destroyed Civilization**: Crocodilian civilization at its peak
- **Rulers**: Archosaurian croc-line masters of rivers and deltas
- **Achievements**: Amphibious, riverine civilization with sophisticated water management
- **Collapse Mechanism**: End-Triassic volcanism and CO₂ chaos destroy them
- **Survivors**: Small proto-dog-like synapsids + early dinosaurs
- **Mythic Hook**: "The Lords of the Rivers drowned in fire, but the dogs howled into the dawn"

## Easter Archetype Significance
- **Most Common Easter Sync**: This extinction most frequently aligns with Easter in calendar calculations
- **Qingming Connection**: Links to Qingming festival (April 4-6) when Easter often falls
- **Central Passion**: Archetypal Easter event - massive destruction enabling resurrection/rebirth
- **30 AD vs 33 AD Parallel**: Historical vs. ritualized dating of Easter celebration

## End-Triassic Extinction Details
- **Global Volcanism**: CO₂ spike and climate chaos
- **Ecosystem Collapse**: Would destroy even robust technological society
- **Archosaur Diversification**: Many experimental branches eliminated, clearing space for dinosaurs
- **Dinosaur Opportunity**: Clears ecological space for dinosaur dominance

- **Post-Extinction Recovery**: Large herbivore herds showing ecosystem rebuilding
- **Social Cooperation**: Herding behavior demonstrates post-crisis social evolution
- **Angiosperm Exploitation**: Duck-billed dinosaurs exploiting flowering plant revolution
- **New World Order**: Dinosaur-dominated ecosystems replacing archosaur experiments
