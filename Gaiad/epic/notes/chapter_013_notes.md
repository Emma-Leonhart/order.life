# Chapter 013 Notes: Solar Nebula Collapse
*Sagittarius 13 - Chapter 13 of 364*

## Chapter Content Summary
This chapter provides a comprehensive survey of our Solar System, describing the formation and characteristics of all the planets from Mercury to the outer Trans-Neptunian objects. Each world is personified as one of Aster's "daughters" with their own evolutionary story and current state.

## Inner Solar System
- **Mercury**: "Once cloaked in waters" but lost liquid due to solar heat, now "rocky orb"
- **Venus**: Struck by impact causing retrograde rotation, "world of heat and clouds" with "sulfur skies"
- **Earth (Gaia)**: "Fair Gaia" with vibrant life, "cradle formed for life's enduring race"
- **Mars**: "Once water flowed in streams" but "lost his dreams," waters now "sunk beneath crimson skies"
- **Ceres**: "Younger sister, late to join the dance" with "beauty in her rocky heart"

## Gas Giant Systems
- **Jupiter**: "Great Jupiter" with major moons Io, Europa, Ganymede in celestial dance
- **Saturn**: Home to icy moons Enceladus and Titan leading "frozen kin"
- **Uranus**: Listed among "gaseous daughters four"
- **Neptune**: Mentioned as part of gas giant family

## Outer Solar System
- **Trans-Neptunian Objects**: "Distant field" where "icy secrets yet remain concealed"
- **Oort Cloud**: "Veil of frozen whispers far from light" where "comets there abound"

## Scientific Accuracy
- **Planetary Formation**: Describes realistic formation from "nebula" and solar gravitational effects
- **Mercury's History**: Accurately describes loss of atmosphere due to solar proximity
- **Venus's Retrograde Rotation**: Correctly attributes to ancient impact event
- **Mars's Water Loss**: Accurate description of ancient Martian rivers and current dry state
- **Io's Volcanism**: "Fires within her heart" and "waters boiled beneath volcanic flame"
- **Outer System Structure**: Correct description of Trans-Neptunian region and Oort Cloud
