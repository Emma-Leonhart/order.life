# Chapter 128 Notes: The Great Split
*Aries 16 - Pre-Human Animal Chapters - Stage V (Primates)*

## Arc Overview
- **Chapter 16 of 17**: Pre-Human Animal Chapters (Cenozoic arc)
- **Stage V**: Primates (Chapters 124-128)
- **Timeline**: Late Miocene-Pliocene (~8-2.6 million years ago)
- **Location**: Africa

## Major Events
### Chimpanzee-Human Divergence
- **6-8 million years ago**: Last common ancestor of chimps and humans
- **Genetic separation**: DNA lineages beginning to diverge
- **Ecological pressure**: Climate change affecting African forests
- **Habitat fragmentation**: Forests shrinking, savannas expanding

### Chimpanzee Evolution
- **Forest specialists**: Maintaining arboreal lifestyle
- **Knuckle-walking**: Specialized ground locomotion
- **Tool use culture**: Termite fishing and nut cracking
- **Fission-fusion society**: Flexible group structures

### Human Lineage Emergence
- **Bipedalism beginnings**: Early experiments with upright walking
- **Savanna adaptation**: Moving toward open habitats
- **Brain expansion**: Gradual increase in cranial capacity
- **Tool innovation**: Stone tool experiments

### Evolutionary Pressures
- **Climate change**: Cooling and drying African climate
- **Food scarcity**: Driving innovative foraging strategies
- **Predator pressure**: Ground living increasing vulnerability
- **Social cooperation**: Enhanced group coordination for survival

## Key Characters
- **Pan**: The Chimpanzee, forest cousin
- **Sahelanthropus**: The Skull of Possibility, early hominid
- **Orrorin**: The Original Man, early bipedal ancestor
- **Ardipithecus**: The Ground Ape, transitional form

## Themes
- Evolutionary divergence from common ancestry
- Environmental pressure driving innovation
- Alternative evolutionary strategies
- Foundation for human consciousness
